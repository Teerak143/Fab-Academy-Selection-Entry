Advanced Kinematics Algorithm

The Advanced Kinematics Algorithm is the set of rules and math that tells the robot how to move. It’s like the "translator" that takes a math equation from a textbook and turns it into actual movements for the motors.

How it Works: From Math to Motion

The computer doesn't just "know" how to draw a circle or a spiral. The algorithm has to break the shape down into tiny pieces:

1. Sampling the Shape:The algorithm looks at a formula (like a spiral) and picks thousands of tiny points along that line.

2. Counting Steps:It calculates exactly how many steps each motor needs to take to get from Point A to Point B. Since we use 1/16 microstepping, it can move in increments as small as 1/80th of a millimeter.

3. Sending Pulses:Once it knows the distance, it sends electrical "pulses" to the motors. If it sends pulses faster, the pen moves faster.

Making Movements Smooth

If a motor starts or stops too fast, the machine will shake, and the drawing will look messy. To fix this, the algorithm uses two clever tricks:

Ramping (Acceleration):Just like a car doesn't go from 0 to 60 mph instantly, the algorithm slowly speeds up the motors at the start of a line and slows them down at the end. This keeps the drawing smooth and prevents the motors from getting stuck.

Constant Speed:For complicated curves, the algorithm makes sure the pen moves at the same speed the whole time. It coordinates the X and Y motors so they work together perfectly—like two people carrying a heavy table together.
