How does it work? The three-step cycle

  • Step 1: Compute (The Brain)

    The ESP32 microcontroller breaks a complex math equation into thousands of tiny $(X, Y)$ coordinate points. This creates a digital "map" for the pen to follow.

  • Step 2: Command (The Nerves)

    The custom circuit board (PCB) translates those digital points into fast electrical pulses. It uses $1/16$ microstepping to tell the motors exactly how much to turn for a smooth, clean line.

  • Step 3: Create (The Body)

    The NEMA 17 motors pull the GT2 belts, moving the pen across the zero-tolerance frame. Because the assembly is so rigid, the pen draws a perfect physical version of the original math.
